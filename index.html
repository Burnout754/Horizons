About Level Up

Where did we get our inspiration?

The Level Up table was conceived when we saw the potential market for a more technical, more functional table football table. Table football is a well-established game which is incredibly easy to pick up, however disparity in player skill can lead to a loss of interest in the game over time. We believed that there were modifications that could be made to improve the existing table design which could improve the interest and engagement of players of all skill levels. By adding ways to bridge the skill gap you can create more engaging games for all players involved and as such the table would get more use.

We performed market research which showed that most people have played table football in the past, but few have ever owned a table themselves. Those surveyed were given a series of prospective feature ideas and the most popular amongst those who have played a lot but not owned a table and those who have owned a table were selected to be implemented in an attempt to tempt new consumers with additional functionality over existing tables while appealing to existing consumers who may be consider upgrading.

What tools were used to produce this website and why?

A few tools were utilised in production of this site. The HTML code was written/modified in Atom editor, a free open-source text code editor. Website Production

Things to consider when making a website

What Makes a Good Website?

A good website is one that can efficiently engage a visitor and deliver information. The website should have a memorable and unique style that suits its subject matter, but the design should always be clean enough that the information present on a given page can easilly be identified. Navigation bars should be logical, and a search function is useful, but for large sites or pages its vital. Text should be a readable size at default zoom and images should be well placed but not overpowering unless they are the focus of a page. Finally, a modern site must be mobile friendly as an increasing amount of the population do almost all internet browsing through mobile devices.


What accessibility considerations must be made?

There are many potential conditions or impairments which change how you produce a webpage. For the visually impaired any images should have alternative text-based descriptions in case they utilise text to sound tools. For those who are colour-blind information should not be placed on the page in conflicting colours that may be indistinguishable for them, and in the same vein text and backgrounds should be dyslexia friendly. For the hearing impaired no vital information should be communicated only through audio, and any videos should have subtitles. For those who may have motor or mobility issues the website should be touch screen navigable (especially important on the mobile version) and in case of photosensitivity the website should not be overly bright and any videos or animations on the page which could trigger a seizure should not auto-play and bare warnings. Finally, for the cognitively impaired it should always be clear what page you are presently on and how to navigate between pages


What is GDPR and how have we considered it?

The GDPR (general data protection regulation) is an EU data protection act which came into effect in May of 2018. The intent was to improve the privacy rights of individuals living in the EU by restricting and regulating how businesses and organisations in the public sector collect and handle people’s data. It was intended as an update to the previous data protection laws listen in the DPA. Specifically, the laws refer to personal data, what being any data which could be used to identify someone, anything from address to religious views or genetic data.

The main changes brought about by the GDPR the immediately affect an individual are twofold. Firstly, webpage visitors should be able to get easy access to a full log off all data that has been collected on them. Secondly, no data may be collected without the explicit consent of the visitor, meaning an active opt in must occur. The GDPR also aims to protect people's data from hacks and data breaches as these become increasingly common. Companies must inform all effected individuals within 72 hours of a data breach and any company over 250 employees must have documentation on what data is being collected, why it is being held, how long it will be held for and how the company is protecting it. Failure to meet these regulations will be met with heavy fines.

In order to comply with these, a website should have a page explaining what data is collected, why and who it will be shared with the option to request the data they have collected on you. At any point where data is being collected there should be an active tick box to show consent to the collection. If data is being continuously collected after opting in there should be an easy and clear way to request to unsubscribe. A security system for data should be put into place with a backup plan of how to contact and inform consumers in the event of a breach. In order to be compliant, we ask for and collect no personal information when browsing our page outside of a contact form wherein personal information is needed in order to respond. We would also have a mailing list which would be opt in and easy to opt out of either through the site or via the emails themselves.

Adjustable Players

Change team formation and model characters for personalisation

The adjustable player formation was one of the most popular ideas put forward in our market research. Each player would be designed to easilly clip on and off the rails by means of a slider system and pin through the middle. The sliders and rails are aligned parallel meaning the majority of the force on the players is perpendicular to the sliders meaning they are at no risk of dethatching during play.

The rails would have a large number of holes in them, allowing for players to be moved between positions on rails and even between other rails to customise the player formations. This adds an extra layer of strategy to the game. Players could also be removed from a more skilled competitors side to produce a handicap to balance the game. These two features contribute to improved engagement with the table for longer periods of time.

This feature also would allow for customizable players. A committed customer could design and produce their own replacement figures with any design they want, and were the concept to take off replacement themed sets could be sold to existing customers.
